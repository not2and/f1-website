Significant Events

The history of Formula 1 is rich with significant events that have shaped the sport. From the first championship in 1950 to the introduction of hybrid engines in 2014, each era has brought its own challenges and triumphs.

  • 1950: First official Formula 1 World Championship, won by Giuseppe Farina.
  • 1977: First turbocharged engine victory (Renault).
  • 1994: Introduction of safety car after tragic accidents.
  • 2004: Michael Schumacher wins 7th world title.
  • 2014: Start of the hybrid era with V6 turbo engines.
  • 2021: Dramatic title fight between Verstappen and Hamilton.

Rule Changes

Over the years, Formula 1 has seen numerous rule changes aimed at improving safety, competitiveness, and sustainability. Key changes include the introduction of safety cars, changes in tire regulations, and the implementation of DRS (Drag Reduction System).

  • Safety: Introduction of the HANS device, halo cockpit protection, and improved crash barriers.
  • Performance: Engine size and fuel restrictions, aerodynamic limitations, and cost caps.
  • Technology: Hybrid power units, energy recovery systems (ERS), and advanced telemetry.

Memorable Moments

Formula 1 is filled with unforgettable moments, from legendary races to dramatic finishes. Iconic drivers like Ayrton Senna, Michael Schumacher, and Lewis Hamilton have left their mark on the sport, creating stories that fans cherish.

  • Senna vs Prost: Intense rivalry in the late 1980s and early 1990s.
  • Schumacher's dominance: Five consecutive titles with Ferrari (2000-2004).
  • Brawn GP's fairytale: Jenson Button and Brawn GP win both titles in 2009.
  • Hamilton's records: Most wins and pole positions in F1 history.

Useful Information

  • Temporada: Normalmente de março a dezembro, com cerca de 20-24 corridas.
  • Pontuação: Os 10 primeiros colocados marcam pontos, sendo 25 para o vencedor.
  • Equipes: Cada equipe tem dois pilotos titulares.
  • Classificação: O grid de largada é definido por uma sessão de qualificação aos sábados.
  • DRS: Sistema que permite abrir uma asa móvel para facilitar ultrapassagens em zonas específicas.
